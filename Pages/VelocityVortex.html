Velocity Vortex

Part of my A-Level Computer Science project developed in C# where I achieved a Grade A overall









































About The Game

As part of my A-Level Computer Science coursework, I designed and developed a top-down 2D arcade-style racing game titled Velocity Vortex in C# making use of the Unity game engine. The game features custom-built physics, real-time collision detection, AI-controlled opponents, and local multiplayer functionality. This project allowed me to learn software development fundamentals, and apply object-oriented programming concepts.

Images

Features

AI Opponents

Computer controlled players powered by the A* pathfinding algorithm.

Local Multiplayer

Up to 4 friends can play locally via keyboard or controller, with AI controlled opponents filling the rest.

Custom Physics

Specifically tweaked to give the game an arcade feel.

Leaderboards

Compete against the times of others or try and beat your own personal best.

In Reflection

What I Learned

This project was a valuable learning experience. I gained hands-on experience using Unity and C# to develop a game from scratch. While I had used C# before, this was by far the most ambitious and challenging project I've taken on.


Challenges I Faced

Throughout this project I developed my prioritization skills. At the beginning, one of the biggest challenges I encountered was managing my time effectively. Balancing game development with completing the written coursework was difficult. I had an expansive vision for what I wanted the game to become, showcasing my creativity and ambition. However, as development of the game progressed, I had to scale back and focus on the core elements of the game, removing some features such as additional game modes and planned obstacles like oil slicks. Adapting my priorities to focus on the core elements of the game in this way allowed me to showcase fundamentals of the game and gives me the opportunity to develop it further in the future.


What I Would Do Differently

This project has given me valuable skills and insight for future projects. If I were to do it again, I would prioritize the core gameplay features: multiplayer, racing mechanics, AI opponents, and obstacles. I would also improve the user interface by simplifying the leaderboard and menu systems, allowing me more time to refine the gameplay. Moving forward, I am excited to apply what I have learned, such as my AI opponents and custom physics, into future projects.
